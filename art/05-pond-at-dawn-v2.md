# 05 — Pond at Dawn (variant 2) — Selected Hero Shot

**Image file:** `05-pond-at-dawn-v2.png`
**In-game moment:** The player's own pond in its normal active state during early morning — the selected hero shot composition
**Screen context:** Marketing, store listing, pitch deck cover, trailer opening frame

## What Is In The Image

Top-down orbital view of a small circular koi pond nestled among mossy stones. The composition is more symmetric than variant 1 — the pond occupies the lower-center of the frame.

A small arched wooden bridge crosses the far side of the pond, running left-to-right. The bridge is simple and traditional: unpainted wood, curved in profile, wide enough for a single person. It rises slightly in the middle. Behind the bridge, the suggestion of a garden path continues into softly obscured background.

A cherry tree branch reaches into the frame from the upper-left corner, its blossoms pale pink against the dawn sky. A few petals drift down toward the water.

Three koi circle slowly at the center of the pond — the same default three (vermilion-white, white-grey, pale gold) seen in variant 1. A folded paper scroll floats near the pond's edge in the lower-left quadrant.

Soft morning mist rises from the water, especially around the bridge. The stones around the pond are covered in moss of varying intensity. The color palette is a slightly warmer dawn than variant 1 — more peach, more golden underlight — with the cherry blossoms providing a secondary accent of pale pink.

## Why This Variant Was Selected As Hero

This composition was chosen over variant 1 as the selected hero shot for marketing materials and store listings for several specific reasons:

- **Stronger silhouette.** The arched bridge provides a distinctive outline that reads clearly at thumbnail size. Variant 1's composition is more open and reads less distinctively when scaled down.
- **Symmetric composition.** The centered framing is more iconic and more suitable for overlay text in localized marketing materials.
- **Cherry branch narrative hook.** The cherry blossoms are an immediately recognizable seasonal signal that communicates "this is a contemplative game rooted in Japanese aesthetic tradition" without a single word of copy.
- **Depth through the bridge.** The bridge creates a clear foreground / midground / background depth that variant 1 lacks.
- **The scroll is prominent.** The floating scroll is positioned more visibly in variant 2, directly communicating to a new viewer that *writing something* is central to this game.

## Game Design Decisions Visible Here

The same design decisions visible in variant 1 apply, plus:

- **Bridges are landscaping items.** Players can purchase and place bridges as cosmetic pond decorations (€1.99–€2.99 tier). They do not affect gameplay mechanics — koi do not "use" the bridge, players cannot walk across it. It is purely aesthetic.
- **The cherry branch is seasonal.** Cherry blossoms appear on certain tree landscaping items during the real-world spring season in the player's hemisphere. A player who places a cherry tree in autumn will see bare branches, not blossoms. This is the same seasons mechanic visible in frame 02 applied to plant life.

## Marketing Notes

This frame will appear:

- As the primary artwork on the App Store and Google Play store listings
- As the cover of the pitch deck for the seed round
- As the opening still of the game's first trailer
- On the landing page of lanternwater.studio
- As the press kit's primary asset

The founders have described this frame in internal documents as "the picture we want to be judged on." If a reviewer is critical of any other single frame, they can adjust. If a reviewer is critical of this specific frame, the founders consider that load-bearing feedback.

## Key Emotional Register

This is the frame meant to make a scrolling user stop scrolling. The goal is *unexpected quiet* in the middle of a high-noise storefront.

## What A Reviewer Might Ask About This Frame

- Does it stop the scroll? Can it survive the context of being surrounded by saturated, high-motion mobile game thumbnails?
- Is the Japanese aesthetic coding too overt, to the point where it closes off audiences who would otherwise be interested?
- Does the pale palette hold up on OLED vs LCD displays at storefront thumbnail sizes?
- Is the cherry blossom signal honest given that the game is year-round and cherry blossoms are seasonal?
- Does this composition work across all four launch market storefronts (Germany, Japan, UK, Brazil) or does it favor one culture's visual expectations?
- Is a second hero shot needed for non-spring launch timing?
