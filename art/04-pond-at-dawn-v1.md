# 04 — Pond at Dawn (variant 1)

**Image file:** `04-pond-at-dawn-v1.png`
**In-game moment:** The player's own pond in its normal active state during early morning
**Screen context:** Default home screen when opening the app in the morning hours

## What Is In The Image

Top-down orbital view of a small koi pond nestled among mossy stones. The composition places the pond roughly in the center-right of the frame, with stone borders curving naturally around the water's edge.

A weathered stone lantern stands at the edge of the pond on the left side, its roof mossy. Around the pond, large lotus leaves float on the water surface in a natural cluster. Three koi circle slowly in the open water at the center: one vermilion-and-white, one white with faint grey markings, and one pale gold. They are swimming in a loose rotation that suggests calm cruising rather than feeding.

A soft morning mist rises from the water's surface, catching the first pale light of dawn. The mist is thick enough to slightly obscure the far side of the pond without hiding it entirely.

Near the pond's edge, a single folded paper scroll floats gently on the water — the player's composed-but-not-yet-released message waiting to be sent.

The color palette is muted dawn: pale peach and rose in the implied sky, moss green for the stones and lotus leaves, dark water for the pond, and the clear vermilion/white/gold of the koi. The light source is low and diffuse. There are no sharp shadows.

## Game Design Purpose

This is one of two candidate hero-shot compositions for marketing and store-listing purposes. It depicts the pond in its default peaceful active state — not a special event, not a celebration, just a quiet morning.

Visible design decisions:

- **Three koi is the tutorial starting count.** New players begin with three koi. As the game progresses they can have more (up to a landscaping-dependent cap), but three is the baseline.
- **Lotus leaves are dynamic landscaping.** Players place and arrange lotus, stones, bridges, lanterns, and other pond elements. This frame shows a relatively sparse, natural arrangement — a player who prefers negative space to ornament.
- **The floating scroll is a *pending release.*** A composed haiku that has not yet been attached to a koi will drift gently on the water near the pond's edge until the player performs the release gesture. It cannot be lost, cannot sink, and cannot be disturbed by koi.
- **Time of day matches real time.** The game renders approximate real-world time of day on the player's device. If a player opens the app in the morning, they see morning light. If they open it at midnight, they see night. Weather conditions (rain, mist, clear) are not tied to real weather — they rotate on a slow game-logic cycle.

## Key Emotional Register

Morning in Between Ponds is intended to feel like *finding the cat already awake and waiting for you.* The pond is patient. It has been waiting. Now you are here.

## What A Reviewer Might Ask About This Frame

- Does the calm sell the genre, or is it too sedate for a storefront thumbnail that needs to compete with high-saturation mobile game art?
- Is the pale palette legible at thumbnail size on device?
- Does the composition work for localized store pages with text overlays?
- Is this variant or variant 2 the stronger hero shot?
