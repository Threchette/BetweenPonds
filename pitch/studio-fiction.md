# Lanternwater Studio — Pitch Backstory

## The Studio

**Lanternwater** is a 6-person independent studio founded in 2025, with offices in Berlin and Kyoto. The team:

- **Hana Watanabe** (Kyoto) — Creative Director. 11 years at Nintendo EPD, worked on Animal Crossing: New Horizons and Ring Fit Adventure. Left to explore what she calls "software that respects silence."
- **Kenji Arakawa** (Kyoto) — Art Director. 8 years at Nintendo EPD, background in traditional sumi-e and contemporary illustration. Trained at Kyoto University of the Arts.
- **Mira Henkel** (Berlin) — Product Lead. Formerly Head of Product at a Berlin wellness app (400k MAU, acquired 2024). Background in cognitive psychology.
- **Paul Ostermann** (Berlin) — Technical Lead. Previously backend lead for a European social app. Built the first prototype of the haiku delivery system as a weekend project in 2025.
- **Yuki Tanaka** (Kyoto) — Sound Designer. Traditional shakuhachi musician, also composes ambient electronic music. First game project.
- **Johanna Kellner** (Berlin) — Community & Trust Lead. Formerly content moderation policy at a European tech company. Joined specifically to design the under-13 safety architecture.

## The Origin Story

The team formed after Hana and Mira met at a design residency in Lisbon in 2024. Both were interested in what they called *post-social software* — products that use the same infrastructure as social media but explicitly reject its affordances. Mira had been sketching a haiku-delivery idea for over a year. Hana had been sketching a koi-pond idea for longer. They merged the two concepts on the second night of the residency.

Kenji joined three months later, bringing the traditional art vocabulary that would become the game's identity. Paul and Yuki joined in early 2025. Johanna joined last, in late 2025, as the first external review of the concept raised serious questions about child safety and cross-border messaging.

## The Cultural Consultancy

Lanternwater has a formal advisory relationship with the **Kyoto Koto Foundation** (fictional), a cultural preservation organization founded in 2019 to support contemporary works that engage with Japanese aesthetic traditions. The foundation has sign-off rights on all Japanese-origin assets in Between Ponds — language, symbolism, seasonal kigo selections, and art-direction choices that reference specific traditional forms.

The foundation's advisory seat is unpaid. In exchange, Lanternwater has committed to:
- Credit the foundation as a permanent co-author in the game's credits
- Share 1% of net revenue with the foundation for as long as the game is in active service
- Provide annual cultural audits open to foundation review
- Accept a foundation veto on any new content that draws on traditional Japanese forms

**Question for the panel**: Is this enough, or is this still a Berlin-led studio using Japanese aesthetics with a ceremonial fig leaf? One of the panel's critical tasks is to evaluate the structural depth of this relationship.

## The Seed Round

Lanternwater is raising €1.8M on a €9M pre-money valuation. The lead prospect is a Berlin-based angel fund specializing in "slow tech" and mental-health-adjacent consumer products. The Kyoto Koto Foundation is not investing but has offered a letter of cultural endorsement for the fundraising materials.

The round is intended to carry the team through:
- 18-month development cycle
- Soft launch in Portugal + New Zealand (test markets, real calendar seasons inverted for hemisphere testing)
- Full launch in Germany, Japan, United Kingdom, Brazil
- First 6 months of live operations post-launch

## What Lanternwater Is Asking The Advisory Panel

The founders have explicitly asked the panel **not to protect their feelings**. They want the hardest possible critique across all 23 domains, because they would rather hear it now than after launch.

Specifically:
1. Should we raise the round at all, or is this concept fundamentally unworkable?
2. If we proceed, which of the hard constraints should we reconsider under pressure?
3. Which of the "dangerous mechanics" (Drift Away, Memorial Pond, The Last Koi) should we cut, which should we strengthen, and which are fine as written?
4. Is our positioning strategy — all three audiences, sequenced — defensible?
5. What are the specific issues in the Japanese framing that would, if uncorrected, make the Japanese launch land badly?
6. What is our realistic legal exposure in each of the four launch markets?
7. Where does the research pipeline itself disagree about what this game even is?
