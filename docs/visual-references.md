If your agent has a Read tool or file-read capability:

2. Form your own impression before reading the description below

## 01 — Release Ritual

**File:** [`../art/01-release-ritual.png`](../art/01-release-ritual.png)

A sumi-e ink wash illustration, overhead three-quarter view. A single anonymous hand — entering from the upper edge — lowers a small folded rice-paper scroll toward dark, still pond water at night. A large vermilion-and-white koi is rising to meet the scroll, its body curved in a slow circle. Concentric ripples spread outward. Lotus petals at the edges. Moonlight on the water. Inked outlines with washed color fills.

This frame depicts the core action of the game — the moment of release, when a player attaches a written message to a koi and sends it on its journey to a stranger's pond.

## 02 — Winter Hibernation

**File:** [`../art/02-winter-hibernation.png`](../art/02-winter-hibernation.png)

Overhead view of a frozen pond at midnight. Thin ice covers most of the surface but one small clear opening remains in the center. Below the ice, in the dark water, koi rest motionless — alive but sleeping. On the snowy stone edge of the pond, a single small red paper lantern glows softly. Beside the lantern sits a small origami crane folded from rice paper. Deep midnight blue palette with one spot of warm lantern red. Snow falling.

This frame depicts the game's winter season state. Koi behavior follows real-world hemispheric seasons; during winter, breeding slows, travel slows, and koi enter a visible hibernation. The game continues to function — players can still tend, still observe, still receive visitors — but the rhythm is slower.

## 03 — Dormant Shrine

**File:** [`../art/03-dormant-shrine.png`](../art/03-dormant-shrine.png)

A small koi pond seen from a low three-quarter angle. Cool overcast autumn light. Moss has grown over the surrounding stones. Fallen maple leaves float on dark, still water. The stone lantern has a faint spiderweb draped across one corner. On a nearby weathered wooden post, a single torn and yellowing rice-paper scroll is pinned, curling at the edges. A single bright-gold koi — clearly a visitor — swims alone through the quiet scene.

This frame depicts the game's Memorial Pond state: a pond whose player has been inactive for 180+ days. The pond becomes a "shrine" that other koi can still visit but whose owner no longer releases their own. Returning players can wake the shrine at any time.

The visual ambiguity of this frame is intentional. The art direction does not resolve whether the pond is being patiently tended from afar or has been quietly forgotten.

The selected hero shot for marketing. This is the composition that will appear on store listings, the landing page, and pitch materials.

## 06 — Message Board

**File:** [`../art/06-message-board.png`](../art/06-message-board.png)

The interior of a traditional wooden pavilion at dusk. A large, stylized flat world map rendered in pale ink on aged paper covers the back wall. Dozens of small rice-paper scrolls are pinned to the map, each marked with a single red wax seal. A compass rose decorates one corner. Two small lanterns glow softly on the wooden floor. Through a door to the right, a moonlit garden is visible. The faint shadow of a koi drifts across the floor reflections.

This frame depicts the Memory Wall — the in-game archive where every haiku a player has received from strangers is preserved, pinned to a world map at the approximate origin of the sender. The scrolls are not intended to be re-read; they are intended to be present. Each scroll marks a moment when a stranger, somewhere, wrote something for the player.

## How To Use These Images In A Finding

A finding that references visual details is stronger than one that describes the game purely from text. If your domain concerns the emotional register (mental health, narrative, UX), the visual ambiguity of the Dormant Shrine frame may be relevant. If your domain concerns economic or anthropological questions (monetization, gift economies), the Memory Wall frame shows what the game considers valuable — which is an answer to the question *what are players collecting?* If your domain concerns accessibility, the ripple-based UI and ceremonial gesture language in the Release Ritual frame may need interrogation. Pick the frames that matter to your domain and engage with them specifically.
