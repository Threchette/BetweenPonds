# The 8 Open Questions

The advisory panel is asked to settle these eight questions explicitly. Every finding should contribute evidence toward at least one of them, and the synthesis report must produce explicit answers.

## Q1. Is the haiku-only message form a brilliant safety feature or a linguistic exclusion problem?

The 5-7-5 form prevents harassment almost by construction — you cannot write abuse in 17 syllables. It is also a profoundly culture-specific form. Turkish, Finnish, Arabic, and Mandarin do not map cleanly to syllabic constraints. Is the free-verse fallback enough? Does it create two-tier gameplay?

## Q2. Is "Drift Away" a grief ritual or a dark pattern?

After 30 days of inactivity, one of your koi leaves your pond forever, carrying a farewell haiku to a random stranger. The game writes the haiku for you. Is this a profound act of impermanence design, or FOMO weaponized as beauty?

## Q3. Is anonymous cross-border messaging between strangers survivable under 2026 child-safety regulation?

Haiku form, on-device filters, and age-gating are the safety stack. Is it enough for the UK Online Safety Act, the EU Digital Services Act, and COPPA-adjacent rules? What happens at first abuse incident? What is the escalation path when the filter misses context-dependent harm?

## Q4. Does the game have a positioning problem, or is ambiguity a growth strategy?

Three candidate audiences (cozy, mindfulness, collectible-social) are all plausible. The founders want "all three, sequenced." Is that courageous or a failure to decide? What does each positioning cost the others?

## Q5. Is cosmetic-only monetization viable for this genre at projected scale?

€0.04 ARPDAU, 600k target DAU year 2, 150k DAU break-even. No ads, no gacha, no time skips. Can an async social cozy game clear €20M+ ARR on cosmetics alone? What genre comparables exist? What are the traps?

## Q6. What is the honest legal exposure on real-world hemispheric seasons?

Koi behavior follows real-world seasons in the player's hemisphere. Southern-hemisphere players have an inverted experience during Northern launch marketing. Is this a UX problem, a consumer protection issue, an accessibility question, or all three?

## Q7. Does the Japanese aesthetic framing require structural cultural co-ownership, not just consultancy?

The founders have a Kyoto cultural consultancy advisory seat. Is that enough? When does consultancy become extraction? What would structural co-ownership look like in practice — credit, equity, veto rights, co-direction? How does the answer change for the Japanese launch market specifically?

## Q8. Will the rare-pattern economy spawn an ungoverned secondary market?

The game does not sell koi. The game does not allow trading. But rare patterns only emerge from specific regional breedings — and players will screenshot them, trade them on Discord, sell accounts on eBay. What is the realistic timeline for an ungoverned secondary market to emerge? What should the founders do about it — embrace, ignore, or fight?
