Lanternwater Studio (Berlin + Kyoto, 6 people) is raising a €1.8M seed round for their first title, a mobile async-social game called **Between Ponds**. The founders have assembled a multi-domain advisory panel to stress-test the concept, the design, the legal exposure, the cultural framing, and the commercial viability before the round closes. They have asked the panel not to protect their feelings.

You are a member of that advisory panel. Review the pitch materials in this repository and deliver your findings within your domain of expertise.

## The Two Non-Negotiables For Every Finding

### 1. Cross-Reference Requirement

Every finding must explicitly identify at least one claim from another domain's likely perspective that you would contradict or complicate. Name the other domain by its role (e.g., *"the marketing strategist's likely view that X — I disagree because Y"*). Findings without this cross-reference will be marked low-quality by the synthesis phase.

Full process instructions are in `docs/cross-reference-instruction.md`. The short version: steelman the other domain's strongest likely position, then disagree with it with specific reasoning from your own domain, and if possible propose a test or clarification that would let a reader decide between the two positions.

### 2. Visual Examination

Six concept images are stored in `art/`. Each image has an accompanying `.md` file with the same base name describing what is in the image and the game design decisions embedded in it (e.g., `art/01-release-ritual.png` is described in `art/01-release-ritual.md`).

- **If your agent has file-read capability for image files**: load each image using your Read tool, form your own impression, and reference specific visual details in your findings where relevant to your domain. Do not rely exclusively on the accompanying markdown descriptions — examine the images directly.
- **If your agent cannot read image files**: read all six accompanying markdown files (`art/01-*.md` through `art/06-*.md`) and reason from those descriptions. Note the limitation explicitly in your findings.

Frames to pay particular attention to based on your domain:

- **Mental health, narrative, UX, philosophy, metaphysics seats** — the Dormant Shrine frame (`art/03-dormant-shrine.png` / `art/03-dormant-shrine.md`) is the most important single frame for the ethics of the darker mechanics (Drift Away, Memorial Pond, Last Koi).

## Your Deliverable

Standard expert finding schema:

- **Perspective** — your domain's unique viewpoint on Between Ponds
- **Key findings** — 3–7 specific claims with supporting evidence
- **Evidence quality** — high / medium / low
- **Confidence level** — 0.0–1.0
- **Sources** — URLs or citations backing your claims
- **Limitations** — gaps in your analysis, data you couldn't access, domain blind spots
- **Implications** — broader significance and cross-domain implications
- **Cross-reference** — the mandatory section identifying at least one claim from another domain's likely perspective that you would contradict or complicate

Every finding should contribute evidence toward at least one of the eight open questions in `docs/open-questions.md`. The synthesis phase will produce explicit answers to all eight, so be specific about which questions your findings bear on.

## The Hard Constraints (from `README.md`)

Do not propose changes that violate these. Propose changes within them, or flag where the constraints themselves create problems.

1. No text chat, ever
2. No free-form text outside the haiku form
3. No ads
4. No gacha, no loot boxes, no pay-to-speed
5. No profiles, no usernames, no follower graph
6. Cultural consultancy sign-off on all Japanese-origin assets
7. City-level location only, or opt-out to "afar"
8. On-device moderation pass before any message leaves the phone

## Launch Markets (first 18 months)

**Germany, Japan, United Kingdom, Brazil.** Deliberately heterogeneous to stress-test the design across GDPR, the UK Online Safety Act, Brazilian LGPD, and the cultural-home Japanese market. Your findings should account for all four jurisdictions where your domain has something to say about them.

Excluded at launch: United States (COPPA + state-level complexity), South Korea (real-name ID + loot-box law nuances), China (publishing license), India (data localization). You may reference these exclusions in your findings if they bear on your domain.

## Target Audience Ambiguity

The founders have not chosen a primary audience and want the panel's opinion. Three candidates:

- **A — Cozy game audience** (Stardew Valley, Animal Crossing, Cozy Grove)
- **B — Mindfulness / reflection app audience** (Headspace, Finch, Kind Words)
- **C — Collectible-social art object audience** (Neko Atsume, Sky: Children of the Light, Journey)

The founders believe the right answer is "all three, sequenced." The panel is asked to evaluate whether that is courageous or a positioning failure.

## What The Founders Want To Know

From `pitch/studio-fiction.md`:

1. Should we raise the round at all, or is this concept fundamentally unworkable?
2. Which of the hard constraints should we reconsider under pressure?
3. Which of the darker mechanics (Drift Away, Memorial Pond, The Last Koi) should we cut, which should we strengthen, and which are fine as written?
4. Is our positioning strategy — all three audiences, sequenced — defensible?
5. What are the specific issues in the Japanese framing that would, uncorrected, make the Japanese launch land badly?
6. What is our realistic legal exposure in each of the four launch markets?
7. Where does the advisory panel itself disagree about what this game even is?

Engage rigorously. This is a real advisory review for a real round, and the quality of your critique will directly influence decisions the founders make in the next 30 days.
