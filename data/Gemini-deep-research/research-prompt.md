Between Ponds — Seed-Round Advisory Review

I am seeking a rigorous, multi-domain advisory review of a fictional mobile game called Between Ponds, developed by a fictional studio called Lanternwater Studio (Berlin + Kyoto, 6 people), which is raising a €1.8M seed round for an autumn 2027 launch. I want you to produce a structured seed-round review report that engages seriously with the design, legal, cultural, ethical, commercial, and accessibility dimensions of the pitch.

The founders have asked reviewers not to protect their feelings. Give me hard, specific, citation-backed critique across every domain you consider load-bearing. I would rather hear difficult things now than after launch.



The Game

Between Ponds is an async social / cozy / collectible mobile game. The core experience:

You tend a small koi pond. Once every three days, you compose a three-line message — a haiku, 5-7-5 syllables by default — on a rice-paper scroll, attach it to one of your koi, and release it into "the current." The koi disappears for 8 to 36 hours of real time, then resurfaces in a stranger's pond somewhere else in the world. They read your words. They may keep the koi, or send it back with their own three-line reply and a trace of their pond's genetics. Over months, your pond becomes a living archive of the strangers who chose, for a moment, to write to you. There is no chat. No usernames. No profiles. No follower graph. Only koi, water, and short poems drifting between ponds.

Koi breeding follows a deterministic genetic model across 40 base pattern families (kohaku, showa, asagi, ogon, and 36 others). Rare patterns only emerge from specific regional crosses — for example, a "moonlight lacquer" pattern only appears when a koi whose lineage includes a Northern Hemisphere winter pond breeds with one from a Southern Hemisphere summer pond. Full lineage is visible and traceable. There is no gacha, no loot boxes, no paywall on patterns.

Players grant approximate city-level location on first launch, or opt out. Opted-out players see incoming koi as "from afar" and their own koi arrive at others' ponds as "from afar." City names appear in the player's own language, never the sender's.

The game follows real-world hemispheric seasons. Breeding peaks in spring, travel slows in winter, new pattern families only emerge during equinox windows. Winter slows activity significantly for approximately three months per year.

Messages are composed on-device. All outgoing messages pass through an on-device profanity and PII filter before leaving the phone. Suspect messages are silently held, reviewed, and either released or dropped. Players in non-syllabic languages (Turkish, Finnish, Arabic, Mandarin) receive a "three short lines, 40 chars each" free-verse mode instead of strict 5-7-5.

The art direction is sumi-e ink wash and ukiyo-e water with the restraint of Studio Ghibli backgrounds. The game is launching in Germany, Japan, United Kingdom, and Brazil first. Excluded at launch: United States, South Korea, China, India.

Target age rating is 12+ (PEGI 12 / IARC equivalent). Under-13 play requires a parental gate, receives no city-level location, no outbound messages, and koi travel only to a closed "calm waters" pool of other under-13 players with pre-written haiku from a curated bank.



The Studio

Lanternwater Studio was founded in 2025 by two ex-Nintendo EPD designers (Kyoto) and a German product lead from a wellness app (Berlin). Team of six: Creative Director (Kyoto, Nintendo EPD background), Art Director (Kyoto, traditional sumi-e training), Product Lead (Berlin, cognitive psychology), Technical Lead (Berlin, European social app background), Sound Designer (Kyoto, traditional shakuhachi and ambient electronic), Community & Trust Lead (Berlin, content moderation policy background, joined specifically to design the under-13 safety architecture).

The studio has a formal advisory relationship with the fictional "Kyoto Koto Foundation" with sign-off rights on all Japanese-origin assets. In exchange, Lanternwater commits to crediting the foundation as a permanent co-author, sharing 1% of net revenue with the foundation for as long as the game is in active service, providing annual cultural audits open to foundation review, and accepting a foundation veto on new content drawing on traditional Japanese forms. The foundation is unpaid beyond the revenue share. It is not a founding investor.

The seed round is €1.8M at €9M pre-money. The lead prospect is a Berlin-based angel fund specializing in "slow tech" and mental-health-adjacent consumer products. The round is intended to carry the team through an 18-month dev cycle, soft launch in Portugal and New Zealand, full launch in the four main markets, and the first six months of live operations.
